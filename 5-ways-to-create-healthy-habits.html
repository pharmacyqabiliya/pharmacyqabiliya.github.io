How to create healthy habits

Adopting a healthy lifestyle has so many benefits. From preventing health problems and living longer to reduced stress and a happier mindset, there’s tons of reasons why making healthier choices is worth it. Changing any habit can be difficult but the good news is that there are plenty of proven ways to help you reach your goal and build a better lifestyle.
In this article, we share five evidence based insights that can help you to create healthy habits that stick.

1. Do it for you

When it comes to setting a goal, we often start by forcing a measurement on ourselves (I HAVE to lose X kg, I SHOULD do X, X person wants me to do Y). But if you want to create changes that you will stick to in the long term, you have to dig for the motives behind why making lifestyle changes is important for you and you alone.

- Ask yourself; “Why is it important to me to create this change in my life?”
- Think about how you want to feel by making the change. For example: “Eating 5 a day, allows me to set a positive example for my children, walking for 20 minutes at lunch gives me the energy to focus on work, a 5-minute meditation helps me feel calm with my family.”

When you create healthy habits based on why they matter to you, what they give you back in your life, those are the changes that stick.

2. Start with little things you enjoy

Why do we pound the pavement or treadmill when we don’t really enjoy it most of the time? Because we think it’s good for us – but it’s not good for us if we can’t stick with it. Research has shown that the healthy habits that we enjoy are the ones that last.

Ask yourself “What healthy habits do I like?”. In order to stick to our healthy habits, we have to enjoy them. It could be:

- Salsa dancing in your kitchen
- Taking the dog or the kids to the park
- How you love the taste of fresh fruit
- You love listening to heavy metal music whilst doing a workout
- You enjoy the quiet time whilst doing yoga or meditation
- Watching your favourite TV show or listening to that podcast you love whilst you chop vegetables for the week’s dinners

Whatever it is, the key is that if you’re enjoying your healthy habits, it makes the rest feel easy.

3. Small changes are powerfull changes

A commonly held habit myth is that big goals lead to big changes.

We have all heard the story of someone who woke up one day and decided that they were going to change - 10 stone or 1 million $ in the bank later, they have transformed.

Whilst this might be true for a small few, the majority will crash and burn when trying to transform ourselves overnight. We have, after all, a life to live:

- A demanding job
- Children to pick up from school
- A parent that needs help
- Meals to prep
- ...the list goes on!

A big goal requires an enormous amount of energy, willpower and focus.

Ways to make a positive change in your daily routine

Instead of trying to do everything at once, why not focus on making one small change and doing that consistently? You could even set a goal to make one bite-sized change each week. Here are some small changes you could try:

- Plan a healthy snack at 4pm to avoid the biscuit slump
- Aim to walk 100 more steps on your fitness tracker each day
- Wake up 30 minutes early to go for a walk or exercise
- Turn your phone off 30 minutes before you go to bed to avoid looking at a blue light
- Add an extra portion of vegetables to your dinner

Each time you achieve it you get a sense of satisfaction and this spurs you on to stick with it. You can then step it up gradually over time.

The research shows smaller, simpler actions become habits more easily. Small is powerful, that's why small changes culminate in big results. Ask yourself: “What one small change can I commit to for the next week?”

How to develop healthy eating habits?
The key to healthy eating is consuming a variety of different foods that ensure your body gets the right amount of nutrients and calories. Eating more than your body needs will result in energy being stored as excess fat. Whilst eating too little will cause you to lose weight.

Follow the below tips for a healthy diet:

- Start the day with a high fibre breakfast such as a low sugar cereal with fruit
- Choose high fibre or wholegrain carbohydrates such as brown rice and wholewheat pasta over white or refined varieties
- Eat at least 5 portions of fruit and vegetables every day
- Include at least 2 portions of fish in your diet per week including 1 serving of oily fish such as salmon, sardines or mackerel
- Swap saturated fat and sugar for healthier alternatives including lean meat, vegetables oils, avocados, honey and naturally sweetened syrups
- Eat no more than 6g of salt a day
- Drink 6 to 8 glasses of water or non-alcoholic beverages each day to avoid dehydration

4. Put your habit in the real world, your world

If you do something at the same time in the same place each day, it’s more likely to become a habit. Do this by asking yourself when and where you are going to perform your small change this week.

Here is how it works:

- Instead of wanting to “exercise more”, change it to “if it's Monday or Wednesday I’ll do an online weights/dance/Pilates class at 7am”
- Instead of “eat healthier this week” change it to, “after lunch on Sunday at 3pm I take 60 minutes to plan my meals/do my shopping/prepare healthy snacks for the coming week”

The key with this is to be specific to your life. You may need to experiment a bit to find the when and where that works best for you but after a couple of weeks you will find your groove and creating that habit will become easier.

5. Celebrate your success

Trying to create healthy habits that last can be hard. Research has found that celebrating them not only speeds up our ability to form healthy habits but also makes the process more fun.

When you celebrate your changes, be it drinking that extra glass of water or taking a 10-minute walk, you tap into the reward circuits of your brain. You essentially train your brain to learn that the activity you performed feels good and makes it much more likely you’ll want to repeat that habit more often and repetition is what leads to long term success.

How do you celebrate? In whatever way helps you feel good about the small change you’ve just made. It could be:

- To sing your favourite song
- Visualise yourself scoring a goal
- Do a drum roll on your desk
- Or say in your head or aloud “I am creating healthy habits that make me feel good”

The key is that you experiment to find the celebration that works for you and then do it immediately after your new healthy habit. Ask yourself: “How can I celebrate my new habit immediately after I’ve done it?” Feeling good about the changes you make will fast track your healthy habits, make you feel good and make it much more likely that those changes will last all of the year ahead (and beyond).

Creating new habits isn’t always easy, but by following the above five tips, you can make healthy changes for good. Take your first step to building healthy habits by improving your diet. You can also find stop smoking services including advice and products that can help you to quit today.
